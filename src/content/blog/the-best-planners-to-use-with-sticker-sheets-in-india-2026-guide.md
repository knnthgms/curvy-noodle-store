You've found the perfect sticker sheets. Now you need a planner that actually works with them. Not all planners are created equal when it comes to stickers — box size, paper quality, and layout all make a difference to how your spreads turn out.

If you're based in India and looking for planners that pair beautifully with sticker sheets, here's our honest guide to the formats and styles worth considering in 2026.

## What Makes a Planner 'Sticker-Friendly'?

Before getting into specific recommendations, here's what to look for in any planner you're considering:

- **Daily box size** — individual daily boxes should be at least 2 cm wide to accommodate most standard sticker characters
- **Paper quality** — thicker paper (80gsm or above) prevents stickers from wrinkling and stops bleed-through from pens used on top of stickers
- **Layout flexibility** — a planner with some open space is better than one packed wall-to-wall with printed text, leaving no room for sticker placement
- **Binding** — lay-flat binding (spiral or sewn) makes placing stickers significantly easier since the planner stays open flat

## Planner Formats That Work Best with Stickers

### A5 Dot Grid Bullet Journals

The classic choice for sticker lovers. A5 is the sweet spot for size — big enough to show off illustrated sticker characters, small enough to carry with you. Dot grid pages give you complete freedom over your layout, so you can place stickers anywhere without fighting against pre-printed lines or boxes.

The [Neorah Dot Notebook](https://atelierneorah.com/products/page-a-day-journal) is another lovely option.

### B6 Daily Planners

Slightly smaller than A5 but with a dedicated daily structure that many people find easier to maintain consistently. B6 planners typically have daily boxes that fit our action and emotion sticker characters perfectly. The Hobonichi Weeks and similar B6 planners from Japanese stationery brands are popular in the Indian planning community.

### Standard Weekly Planners

If you prefer a more structured approach, a weekly planner with clearly defined daily columns is a great companion for activity and tracker stickers. Place an activity sticker at the start of each day's column to visually map out your week before filling in the details.

Look for weekly planners with a notes section — this is the perfect space for a mood tracker sticker grid.

## What to Avoid

- **Planners with very small daily boxes** — anything less than 1.5 cm wide will make most sticker characters look cramped or get cut off
- **Coated or glossy paper** — stickers adhere well but pens slide and smear on glossy surfaces, which is frustrating when writing on top of or around stickers
- **Very thin paper (less than 70gsm)** — stickers can cause slight wrinkling on lightweight pages
