People sometimes ask if our stickers are "really" handmade, since the
final product is printed. Fair question! Here's the honest, complete
journey of every sticker in the shop, from first pencil line to your
mailbox.

## It starts in a sketchbook

Every design begins as a pencil doodle, usually several pages of them.
Ramen Buddy went through eleven versions before the noodles got curvy
enough. (Version three looked alarmingly like spaghetti. We don't speak of
version three.)

The sketch stage is where the personality happens — the tilt of an
eyebrow, how closed a sleeping eye should be. We've learned that "almost
symmetrical" is cuter than "symmetrical", so we keep the wobble.

## Inking and colour

The chosen sketch gets scanned and redrawn digitally with a brush that
mimics a slightly-too-soft pencil. Then comes colour: our palette of
cream, peach, sage, warm pink and noodle-yellow, picked so that any two
stickers look like siblings on the same laptop lid.

### Why pastel?

Honestly? Because loud neon stickers shout, and we want ours to hum. Soft
colours sit politely next to your handwriting instead of competing with it.

## Printing in small batches

We print on waterproof vinyl in small batches — usually 50 sheets at a
time. Small batches mean we can check every single sheet for colour
accuracy, retire designs that aren't sparking joy, and try slightly
ridiculous experiments (shimmer laminate was one such experiment; it
stayed).

Each sheet gets a laminate layer, matte or glossy depending on the design.
That laminate is what makes the stickers survive water bottles, dishwasher
mishaps and monsoon season.

## Cut, checked, packed by hand

Stickers are kiss-cut and then weeded, checked and packed by hand. Yes,
that's slower. It's also how we catch the one-in-fifty misprint, and how
every envelope ends up with a hand-drawn doodle on the flap.

## The last step is you

The honest answer to "are they really handmade?" is: a sticker is drawn by
hand, refined by hand, checked by hand and packed by hand — the printer
just does the photocopying in the middle. The very last step of the
process is you, sticking it somewhere it'll make an ordinary Tuesday a
little better. That part we can't do without you.
