If you are new to the planning space, finding a planner that meets your needs can be tricky. What are you supposed to look for?

- Page quality?
- A local or international brand?
- Something plain and simple or something pretty?

Needs vary based on preferences; as a pro planner having over 10 years of experience using such products, here's what I think can be a good starting point for anybody looking to purchase a planner.

## 1. Meeting your needs

This varies from person to person. In essence, a planner should make sense to you. For some people, looking only for a basic high-level overview, a monthly grid may make sense. For those who enjoy long-form writing and task noting, a daily planner would be a good fit. For others who want a completely customisable option, bullet journaling could be what works.

## 2. Identifying a size

There are many sizes for planners, and the right size makes such an absolute difference. You would need to identify where you see yourself using your planner the most. Your desk, on-the-go, or at your work? Even the size of your desk may matter, so identifying a size from A6 to A4 and B6 to B5 is important.

## 3. Thinking about your style

Would you be okay with a simple black and white way of planning, or do you want to add pictures, stickers, and ephemera?

These are some of the very basic items to think of before purchasing a planner, because as a start, it will give you a lot of clarity and make a choice that could be close to perfect.
