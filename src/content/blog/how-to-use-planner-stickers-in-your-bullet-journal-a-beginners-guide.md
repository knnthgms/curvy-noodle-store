## Why Planner Stickers Are a Game-Changer

Ask anyone who uses stickers in their planner regularly — they'll tell you it changed their whole relationship with planning. Here's why:

- **Speed**: Instead of hand-lettering 'exercise' or drawing a tiny dumbbell icon for the fifth time this week, you peel a sticker. Done.
- **Consistency**: A visual system using stickers is easier to maintain because it looks good even on your lowest-effort days.
- **Mood tracking**: Emotion stickers help you spot patterns — like noticing that Mondays are consistently an 'anxious' day, or that gym days correlate with a happier mood by evening.
- **Motivation**: There's something genuinely satisfying about placing a 'completed' or 'done' sticker after finishing a task.

## Types of Planner Stickers and What They're For

Not all stickers serve the same purpose. Here's a breakdown of the main types, and exactly how to use each one.

### Emotion Stickers

Emotion stickers feature illustrated characters showing a range of feelings — happy, tired, sick, anxious, motivated, sad, and more. Use them in your daily log to record how you felt at the end of each day, or place them in a dedicated mood tracker grid for a visual month-at-a-glance view of your emotional patterns.

Pro tip: place the sticker at the end of the day, not the beginning. You're tracking how the day actually went, not how you hoped it would go.

### Activity & Action Stickers

These stickers show characters doing daily tasks — cooking, working, exercising, cleaning, napping, studying, and more. Use them in your weekly spread to mark what's on your schedule, or in your habit tracker to visually log whether you did the activity that day.

This works especially well for habit tracking. Sticking a cooking character on a day you meal-prepped feels far more rewarding than ticking a box.

### Tracker Stickers

Tracker sticker sheets are designed specifically for habit, mood, and goal tracking. They often include pre-formatted boxes, grids, and labels that save you from setting up trackers from scratch. Perfect for journaling beginners who love the idea of a bullet journal but don't yet want to commit to drawing out layouts by hand.

## How to Place Stickers in Your Planner (Step by Step)

Setting up your planner with stickers is part art, part system. Here's a simple approach that works whether you use a structured daily planner or a free-form bullet journal:

1. **Plan your week on paper first.** A light pencil sketch of your weekly spread helps you know where you're placing things before committing.
2. **Add functional stickers first.** Place activity and tracker stickers in their relevant spots before decorating.
3. **Fill in your actual tasks and appointments** around the stickers.
4. **Add emotion stickers at the end of each day**, not at the start.
5. **Review your spread at the end of the week.** You'll start noticing patterns in your mood, energy, and activity that you never noticed before.
