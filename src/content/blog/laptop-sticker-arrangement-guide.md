A laptop lid is prime real estate: you'll look at it every day for years,
and so will everyone behind you in every café and lecture hall. No
pressure! Okay, some pressure. Here's how to arrange stickers you won't
regret.

## Decide your density first

Laptop sticker styles roughly fall on a spectrum:

- **The minimalist** — one to three stickers, generous spacing, lots of
  visible lid. Calm, intentional, very "I have my life together".
- **The curated cluster** — five to ten stickers grouped in one region,
  usually a corner or around the logo. Our personal favourite.
- **The full chaos collage** — every centimetre covered, stickers
  overlapping stickers. Glorious, but commit fully; a half-finished
  collage reads as "lost steam in 2024".

Pick your destination *before* you stick anything. Most regret comes from
minimalists accidentally drifting into chaos one sticker at a time.

## Plan with paper first

Cut paper rectangles roughly the size of your stickers and arrange those
with removable tape. Live with the layout for a day. This sounds fussy,
but it's the difference between "placed" and "stuck on in a hurry before
class".

### The triangle trick

When in doubt, arrange your three favourite stickers in a loose triangle
with breathing room between them. Triangles feel balanced from every
angle, which matters for an object that's viewed upside-down half the time
(remember: people across the table see your lid inverted).

## Mind the practicalities

A few unglamorous tips from experience:

1. **Clean the lid with rubbing alcohol** before sticking — skin oils are
   the main reason stickers peel early.
2. **Stay clear of the edges** by about a centimetre, where sleeves and
   bags rub the most.
3. **Waterproof vinyl only** if your laptop shares a bag with a water
   bottle. (All of ours is, for exactly this reason.)

## When to break every rule

If a sticker makes you grin, it goes on the laptop, layout be damned. The
best laptop lids aren't designed; they're accumulated, one small joy at a
time, until one day a stranger at a coffee shop says "I love your
stickers" and the whole system is validated.
