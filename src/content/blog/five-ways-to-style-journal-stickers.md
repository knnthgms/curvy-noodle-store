If you've ever held a sticker over a blank page for a full minute, frozen,
because *what if you waste it* — this post is for you. Here are five
layouts we use constantly, none of which require artistic confidence.

## 1. The header anchor

Place one sticker at the start of your page title, like a bullet point
that went to art school. That's it. That's the technique.

A food or animal sticker at 45 degrees instantly makes a plain weekly
spread feel intentional. If your handwriting wobbles (whose doesn't?), the
sticker draws the eye and the wobble disappears.

## 2. The mood corner

Reserve the top-right corner of each daily page for one sticker that sums
up the day. Slurpy noodle bowl for cosy days, dramatic fern for dramatic
days. After a month you have a tiny graphic novel of your moods — way more
fun to flip back through than a rating out of ten.

## 3. The washi bridge

Stickers and washi tape are best friends. Run a strip of washi along the
page edge, then overlap a sticker so it sits half on the tape, half on the
page. The overlap is the trick: it looks layered and deliberate, like a
collage, even when it took four seconds.

## 4. The habit tracker mascot

Pick a sticker to "host" each habit tracker. A water-drinking tracker
hosted by a boba cup is funnier and weirdly more motivating. You're not
filling in a grid for yourself — you're doing it so the boba cup is proud
of you.

### Make it seasonal

Rotate the mascot monthly. The moon for a sleep tracker in winter, plants
for a spring decluttering streak. Tiny ritual, big serotonin.

## 5. The full-bleed corner cluster

For title pages and monthly covers: cluster three stickers of different
sizes in one corner, letting one run slightly off the page edge (fold the
overhang to the back, or trim it). Odd numbers and overlapping edges are
what make clusters look "designed" — that's the entire secret.

## Stop saving your stickers

Real talk: a sticker in a drawer brings joy to no one. The best layout is
the one that gets the sticker out of the drawer and onto a page you'll
smile at later. You can always order another noodle — that's literally why
the shop exists.
